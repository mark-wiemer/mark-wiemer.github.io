\(\newcommand{\dwrt}{\mathrm{d}} % "derivative with respect to" \newcommand{\integral}[2]{\int {#1} \,\dwrt{#2}}\) Suppose we have $$ I_n = \integral{\cos^n(x)}{x} $$ where $n$ is some whole number: $n \in \{0, 1, 2, \dots\}$ We can simplify this with integration by parts: $$ \integral{\cos^n(x)}{x} = \integral{\cos^{n - 1}(x)\cos(x)}{x} $$ $$ u = \cos^{n - 1}(x), \; \dwrt{v} = \cos(x) \,\dwrt{x}$$ We can solve for $\dwrt{u}$ using the Chain Rule. Recall that for any integer $k \neq -1$, $\cos^k(x) = [\cos(x)]^k$. (We exclude $-1$ because $\cos^{-1}(x) = \arccos(x)$) \begin{align*} u &= \cos^{n - 1}(x) \\ u &= [\cos(x)]^{n - 1} \\ \dwrt{u} &= (n - 1)[\cos(x)]^{n - 2} \cdot [-\sin(x)] \,\dwrt{x} \\ \dwrt{u} &= -(n - 1)\cos^{n - 2}(x)\sin(x) \,\dwrt{x} \end{align*} And $v$ is simple: $v = \integral{}{v} = \integral{\cos(x)}{x} = \sin(x)$ Substituting in our functions, we have: \begin{align*} \integral{u}{v} &= uv - \integral{v}{u} \\ &= \cos^{n - 1}(x)\sin(x) - \integral{\sin(x)\left[-(n - 1)\cos^{n - 2}(x)\sin(x)\right]}{x} \\ &= \cos^{n - 1}(x)\sin(x) + \integral{\sin(x)(n - 1)\cos^{n - 2}(x)\sin(x)}{x} \end{align*} For simplicity, I will introduce a new variable $A = \cos^{n - 1}(x)\sin(x)$. \begin{align*} I_n &= \cos^{n - 1}(x)\sin(x) + \integral{\sin(x)(n - 1)\cos^{n - 2}(x)\sin(x)}{x} \\ &= A + \integral{\sin(x)(n - 1)\cos^{n - 2}(x)\sin(x)}{x} \\ &= A + (n - 1)\integral{\sin(x)\cos^{n - 2}(x)\sin(x)}{x} \\ &= A + (n - 1)\integral{\sin^2(x)\cos^{n - 2}(x)}{x} \end{align*} Now recall $\sin^2(x) = 1 - \cos^2(x)$. We can make this substitution and then distribute: \begin{align*} I_n &= A + (n - 1)\integral{\sin^2(x)\cos^{n - 2}(x)}{x} \\ &= A + (n - 1)\integral{\left[1 - \cos^2(x)\right]\cos^{n - 2}(x)}{x} \\ &= A + (n - 1)\integral{\cos^{n - 2}(x) - \cos^2(x)\cos^{n - 2}(x)}{x} \\ I_n &= A + (n - 1)\integral{\cos^{n - 2}(x) - \cos^{n}(x)}{x} \\ \end{align*} We can split the integral on the right-hand side into two parts, and we will see that one of the new integrals on the right-hand side is the same as our integral on the left-hand side. Don't forget the grouping symbols! We need to remember to distribute the $(n - 1)$. \begin{align*} I_n &= A + (n - 1)\integral{\cos^{n - 2}(x) - \cos^{n}(x)}{x} \\ &= A + (n - 1)\left[\integral{\cos^{n - 2}(x)}{x} - \integral{\cos^{n}(x)}{x}\right] \\ &= A + (n - 1)\integral{\cos^{n - 2}(x)}{x} - (n - 1)\integral{\cos^{n}(x)}{x} \\ I_n &= A + (n - 1)\integral{\cos^{n - 2}(x)}{x} - (n - 1)I_n \end{align*} To simplify, we add $(n - 1)I_n$ to both sides and divide by the new coefficient on the left-hand side: \begin{align*} I_n &= A + (n - 1)\integral{\cos^{n - 2}(x)}{x} - (n - 1)I_n \\ I_n + (n - 1)I_n &= A + (n - 1)\integral{\cos^{n - 2}(x)}{x} \\ (1 + n - 1)I_n &= A + (n - 1)\integral{\cos^{n - 2}(x)}{x} \\ nI_n &= A + (n - 1)\integral{\cos^{n - 2}(x)}{x} \\ I_n &= \frac{1}{n}A + \frac{(n - 1)}{n}\integral{\cos^{n - 2}(x)}{x} \\ \integral{\cos^n(x)}{x} &= \frac{1}{n}\cos^{n - 1}(x)\sin(x) + \frac{(n - 1)}{n}\integral{\cos^{n - 2}(x)}{x} \end{align*} Since the resulting integral on the right-hand side is $I_{n - 2}$, our reduction formula is complete.
